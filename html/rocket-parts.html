Engineering breakdown

What each major rocket part does

A launch vehicle is a system of connected components. Each part must survive intense forces while contributing to guidance, fuel storage, propulsion, or payload protection.

Page focus

  • Payload and fairing
  • Fuel tanks and engines
  • Guidance, heat shielding, and staging

Detailed component guide

Payload system

The payload is the mission cargo. It might be a satellite, scientific instrument, cargo capsule, or crew module. Fairings and payload bays protect it during ascent.

Fuel tanks

Propellant tanks store the fuel and oxidiser needed for combustion. Tank design influences mass, structural strength, and how efficiently the rocket can fly.

Rocket engines

Engines convert chemical energy into thrust by accelerating exhaust gases. Their efficiency and reliability strongly shape mission performance.

Guidance system

Computers, sensors, and navigation equipment adjust the flight path in real time. Small guidance corrections can prevent major trajectory errors later in flight.

Heat shield and staging

Heat shields protect spacecraft during re-entry, while staging removes empty sections to reduce mass and improve overall launch efficiency.
