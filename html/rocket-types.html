Comparative study

Rocket propulsion systems in one table

Different rockets solve different mission problems. Some optimise for thrust, some for simplicity, and some for efficiency during long deep-space journeys.

How to read the table

  • Compare fuel choice and mission role.
  • Use the fact column for memorable revision points.
  • Review images alongside the written data.
A simplified comparison of rocket types used in education, launch systems, and advanced mission concepts.
Rocket type Main propellant or system Typical use Key fact Image
Liquid propellant rocket Liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen Crewed launches and orbital missions Can be throttled and shut down during flight.
Solid propellant rocket Solid fuel with oxidiser mixed in Launch boosters and missile systems Reliable and powerful, but harder to control after ignition.
Hybrid rocket Solid fuel with liquid or gas oxidiser Experimental vehicles and research Separating fuel and oxidiser can improve handling safety.
Cryogenic rocket Super-cooled hydrogen and oxygen Upper stages and heavy-lift missions Requires insulation because the propellants are stored at extremely low temperatures.
Reusable rocket Liquid propellant with recovery hardware Commercial launches and cargo services Reusability helps lower the cost per mission.
Ion propulsion Xenon gas accelerated electrically Deep-space probes Produces very low thrust but excellent long-term fuel efficiency.
