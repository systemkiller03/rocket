Historic timeline

Famous launches that shaped space history

Each launch in this short timeline marks a change in how humanity uses rockets: first orbit, first Moon landing, robotic Mars exploration, and reusable launch systems.

Timeline themes

  • Space race milestones
  • Scientific observation missions
  • Modern reusable launch development
1957

Sputnik 1

The Soviet Union launched the first artificial satellite, beginning the space age and proving that orbital flight was possible.

1969

Apollo 11

NASA achieved the first crewed Moon landing, showing that heavy-lift rockets could support human deep-space missions.

1990

Hubble Space Telescope deployment

Hubble transformed astronomy by sending high-resolution images from above Earth’s atmospheric distortion.

2004

Mars rovers Spirit and Opportunity

These missions extended scientific study of Mars and demonstrated how rockets support planetary robotics.

2022

Artemis I

Artemis I helped re-establish a pathway toward long-duration lunar missions and future crewed deep-space exploration.

2020s

Reusable launch era

Modern launch companies have normalised booster recovery and rapid reuse, changing the economics of access to orbit.
